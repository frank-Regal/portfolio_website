My Story
Currently I am a Graduate Research Assistant at The University of Texas at Austin, in the Nuclear and Applied Robotics Group (NRG), with Dr. Mitch Pryor. Here I am pursuing my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering with a focus in Robotics. I completed my Master of Science (2019) and Bachelor of Science (2019) in Mechanical Engineering at Drexel University.

The objective of my current research is to develop advanced systems for human-robot interactions. The goal is to advance the way we humans interact with robots to enable us to do more with less. I envision a world where one human can perform physical work across all seven continents from the comfort of their living room. Hence, a single operator can ultimately teleport across the world within seconds and be the mobile robotic platform they would like and control robotic arms just as if they jumped on a plane and flew.

In addition to research, I enjoy engineering various parts with my 3D printers, following both the american supercross and motocross series, and spending time outdoors traveling, hiking, biking, and camping. I grew up in a very small town, Richmondale, PA, just outside of Scranton (yes like the office) where I spent most of my childhood working with my dad at the family plumbing business, Regal Plumbing & Heating. After graduating high school I began my migration south and moved to Philadelphia, PA for five years where I obtained my B.S. and M.S. I moved to Houston, TX after graduation for a full-time engineering roll in the petro-chemcial industry. After two years in industry I decided to head back to school to solve more challening problems and gain a better understanding of robotics and how they can be used to help us in the future.
